Acropolis of Athens

The Acropolis is the crowning jewel of Athens and one of the most important cultural sites in the world. This incredible, sprawling complex was the birthplace of democracy and the most important center of ancient Greece.

Meteora

The Greek word Meteora means “suspended in the air” and this phrase aptly describes these remarkable Greek Orthodox monasteries. Many centuries ago, on these gigantic rocks with the breathtaking landscape reaching heights of more than 600 meters.

Parthenon

The Parthenon is a temple to Athena in Athens, and the word comes from a word meaning "women's chamber", itself coming from "παρθένος" meaning "unmarried woman".

Temple of Olympian Zeus

The Temple of Zeus at Olympia was built sometime between 472 and 456 B.C. Dedicated to the king of the gods, Zeus, it was built upon a much older religious site going back to the tenth or ninth century.

Panathenaic Stadium

TIL the Panathenaic Stadium, built in 330BC, is the only stadium made entirely of marble, was excavated and used in the 1896 Olympics, and and is still used for events today. Its record attendance of 80,000 was for a basketball game in 1968.
