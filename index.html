Greece : The Land of Democracy.

Greece, officially the Hellenic Republic, is a country in Southeast Europe. Located on the southern tip of the Balkan peninsula, Greece shares land borders with Albania to the northwest, North Macedonia and Bulgaria to the north, and Turkey to the east.



Greece is considered the cradle of Western civilisation, being the birthplace of democracy, Western philosophy, Western literature, historiography, political science, major scientific and mathematical principles, theatre, and the Olympic Games.

Important locations in Greece

Tourism has been a key element of the economy and one of the most important sectors, contributing 21% of gross domestic product in 2018. Greece was the 9th most visited country in the world in 2022, hosting 28 million visitors, an increase from 18 million tourists in 2007.

Most visitors come from the European continent, while the most from a single nationality are from the United Kingdom, followed by Germany. The most visited region of Greece is Central Macedonia.


Acropolis of Athens The Acropolis is the crowning jewel of Athens and one of the most important cultural sites in the world. This incredible, sprawling complex was the birthplace of democracy and the most important center of ancient Greece.
Meteora The Greek word Meteora means “suspended in the air” and this phrase aptly describes these remarkable Greek Orthodox monasteries. Many centuries ago, on these gigantic rocks with the breathtaking landscape reaching heights of more than 600 meters.
The Parthenon is a temple to Athena in Athens, and the word comes from a word meaning "women's chamber", itself coming from "παρθένος" meaning "unmarried woman".
The Temple of Zeus at Olympia was built sometime between 472 and 456 B.C. Dedicated to the king of the gods, Zeus, it was built upon a much older religious site going back to the tenth or ninth century.
The Panathenaic Stadium, built in 330BC, is the only stadium made entirely of marble, was excavated and used in the 1896 Olympics, and and is still used for events today. Its record attendance of 80,000 was for a basketball game in 1968.
